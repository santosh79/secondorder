On Bitcoin

Bitcoin isn’t really about money.

It’s about something far more elemental: the need for an anchor in a world that no longer trusts its mirrors.

We live in a time where experiences can be manufactured, signals are flooded, and truth is often just a version with the best distribution. Amid all this, what humans crave — perhaps more than innovation — is something unchanging. Something that holds still while the world spins.

The 21 million supply limit is not just code.
It’s a collective vow — the perversion of money stops with me. With us. With the network.
That’s not decentralization for its own sake.
That’s anchoring at scale.

Bitcoin reminds us that trust, once lost, isn’t restored through marketing or governance.
It’s rebuilt through math, time, and immovability.

Not hype. Not noise.
Just signal — and patience.

And underneath it all: a quiet return to something we used to prize more freely —
honor.
